# User Personas

## 1. The Athlete

### Goal

Look legitimate, get exposure, update stats quickly, and share performance moments.

### Needs

- Beautiful public profile.
- Fast stat updates.
- YouTube highlights.
- Share cards.
- Follow/reaction feedback.
- Verification or credibility signals.

### Pain points

- Existing stats are scattered.
- Profiles are ugly or incomplete.
- Updating stats feels like admin work.
- Highlights and stats are not connected.

## 2. The Sports Lover

### Goal

Follow athletes, teams, big games, and fresh stats.

### Needs

- Fresh homepage feed.
- Follow athletes and teams.
- Live spectator mode.
- Rankings and search.
- Reactions and sharing.

### Pain points

- Local sports discovery is fragmented.
- Stats are hard to find.
- Social platforms lack structured sports context.

## 3. The Team Manager / Scorekeeper

### Goal

Capture accurate stats quickly and help athletes maintain better profiles.

### Needs

- Live game capture.
- Offline support.
- Bulk roster/stat tools.
- Fast player switching.
- End-game reconciliation.
- Dispute handling.

### Pain points

- Spreadsheets are slow.
- Scorekeeping apps are ugly or complex.
- Live action moves too quickly.
- Multiple people may record different results.

## 4. The Broadcaster / Content Helper

### Goal

Add narrative, highlight markers, and context during live games.

### Needs

- Timeline notes.
- Highlight tags.
- Player/game tagging.
- Spectator-ready updates.

### Pain points

- Stats alone do not tell the story.
- Highlight moments get lost after the game.

## 5. The Admin

### Goal

Control teams, resolve disputes, manage imports, and protect trust.

### Needs

- Admin queue views.
- Claims review.
- Dispute resolution.
- Sports config management.
- Source/import monitoring.
